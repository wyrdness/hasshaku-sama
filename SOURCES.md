## Notes on Source Reliability

### Primary Internet Sources
The original 2008 2channel post is the canonical primary source for Hasshaku-sama. All other accounts derive from this source or are subsequent elaborations. The original thread has been archived on multiple platforms but identifying the specific original poster is impossible due to 2channel's anonymous structure.

### Academic Context
While Hasshaku-sama herself has limited direct academic scholarship due to recent origin (2008), the extensive folklore, urban legend, and internet culture studies provide crucial contextual understanding for analyzing this modern legend.

### Cross-Cultural Comparative Studies
Sources on similar entities (Slender Man, La Llorona, Yama-uba) provide valuable comparative framework for understanding Hasshaku-sama's place in global folklore traditions.

### Japanese Language Materials
Many sources listed exist only in Japanese and provide cultural context not available in English-language scholarship. Translations and interpretations should be approached carefully.
